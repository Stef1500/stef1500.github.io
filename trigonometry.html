Trigonometry

Trigonometry is a mathematical discipline originating from ancient Greece, focusing on the relationships between angles and side lengths of triangles. Its development in the 3rd century BC, spurred by applications in astronomy, led to the study of trigonometric functions which define these relationships through ratios of triangle sides. Greek mathematicians initially concentrated on chord calculations, while Indian scholars produced early trigonometric tables.


Over time, trigonometry found practical applications in fields like geodesy, surveying, celestial mechanics, and navigation due to its ability to model and solve geometric problems involving angles and distances. One of its defining features is the set of trigonometric identities, extensively used to simplify expressions, transform equations, and solve problems in various contexts. These identities enable mathematicians and scientists to manipulate trigonometric functions effectively, making them invaluable in both theoretical and applied mathematics.
